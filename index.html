Welcome & Introduction
Thank you so much for taking the time to be here today. This is a testimonial interview — think of it as a relaxed conversation about your internship experience at TRG. There are no right or wrong answers, so just speak freely and share whatever feels true to you.
Why we do this
Feedforward First and foremost, your honest feedback helps us understand what's working well and what we can do better — so future interns have an even stronger experience.
Beyond that…
Professional Development This is also a great opportunity for you to pause and reflect on the skills and knowledge you've built during your time here — something that will come in handy when you talk to future employers.
And finally…
Marketing With your permission, your testimonial may be used to share the program's story and inspire the next generation of interns to apply.

Part 3 — Diversity & Inclusion
3.1 What does Diversity mean to you? ▼
If they freeze or say "I'm not sure"
Think about any group you've been part of — a class, a sports team, a club. What made the people there different from each other?
Is diversity only about things like nationality or appearance — or do you think it goes deeper than that?
Start from a memory, not a definition — almost everyone can describe a diverse group they've been in, even if they can't define the word.
3.2 How do you feel about the concept of Diversity? ▼
If they say "it's important" and trail off
When you're working or studying with people who are very different from you — does that feel energizing, a little uncomfortable, or both at once?
Has your sense of what diversity means actually shifted since joining TRG?
Normalizing "both" (energizing AND uncomfortable) makes it safe to give an honest, nuanced answer.
3.3 Do you think TRG is Diverse? ▼
To get past "yes/no"
What gave you that impression — was it the people on your team, the broader company, or the overall culture?
Compared to your university or other places you've been — where would you put TRG on the spectrum?
3.4 What does Inclusion mean to you? ▼
If they mix it up with Diversity or go quiet
Here's a way to think about it: Diversity is being invited to the party — Inclusion is being asked to dance. Does that resonate?
Think of a time you felt truly heard in a group — like your opinion actually mattered. What made that happen?
Do you think a team can be diverse but still not feel inclusive? What would that look like?
"Diversity is being invited to the party — Inclusion is being asked to dance." Use this if they're stuck — it almost always unlocks the conversation.
3.5 How do you feel about the concept of Inclusion? ▼
If they give a safe, surface-level answer
Have you ever been in a situation — at school, at work, anywhere — where you felt like you weren't quite part of the group? What did that feel like?
In a perfect world, what does an inclusive workplace actually look like on a normal Tuesday — not in theory, but in practice?
The first question gives them permission to share a real experience. The "normal Tuesday" framing makes the second one concrete and easy to picture.
3.6 Do you think TRG is Inclusive? ▼
To get a real story, not just a rating
Can you think of a specific moment at TRG where you felt like your voice genuinely mattered?
When you first arrived — as an intern, maybe from another country — did it take time to feel like you belonged, or did the team make that happen quickly?
The second question is powerful for international interns — it invites a personal story almost automatically.
3.7 Do you think that TRG is gender balanced? ▼
If they're not sure
Just think about the people you interact with most — your team, your manager. What's your gut feeling about the balance?
How does that compare to your university, or other companies you've heard about in your field?
3.8 Does it surprise you that we are gender balanced? Why? ▼
If they say "yes" but don't know why
What were you expecting when you joined — did you picture a more male-dominated room, or did you not think about it at all?
Where does that expectation come from — the industry, your home country, or just what you've seen elsewhere?
3.9 Does it surprise you that your team is more women/men? ▼
To move past surprise into reflection
How has working with a team that's mostly [women/men] compared to other teams or groups you've been part of?
Have you noticed anything about how this team communicates or works together that feels different — in a good way or otherwise?
3.10 How valuable is the TRG diversity & inclusion experience for you? ▼
If they say "very valuable" without more detail
If you had to name one thing — one moment or shift in thinking — that this experience gave you, what would it be?
On a scale of 1 to 10, how much were you thinking about diversity and inclusion before this internship? And where are you now?
The 1–10 scale is low-pressure, quick, and almost always produces a genuine and memorable answer on camera.
